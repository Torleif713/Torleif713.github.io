Mimicing African American Activists: How Russia Tried To Supress The Black Vote.

Read Time: 5 minutes

In the 2016 U.S. elections, Russian puppet accounts, controlled by the Internet Research Agency (IRA), specifically targeted African Americans. These accounts spread disinformation to suppress voter turnout, amplify racial tensions, and exploit social issues. By posing as activists, they aimed to manipulate public opinion and sow division within the African American community.

Author: David Brand

The Targeting of Right-Leaning Americans Through Russian Puppet Accounts

Read Time: 5 minutes

In the 2016 U.S. elections, right-leaning Russian-controlled accounts linked to the Russian Internet Research Agency (IRA) spread disinformation aimed at influencing voters. These accounts, posing as American voices, amplified divisive political content, pushing false narratives and fueling polarization to undermine trust in the democratic process and favor certain candidates.

Author: David Brand

Statistics On The Reach Of Russian Facebook, Instagram And Twitter Accounts

Read Time: 5 minutes

In the 2016 U.S. elections, Russian accounts linked to the Internet Research Agency (IRA) flooded Facebook, Instagram, and Twitter with disinformation. On Facebook alone, over 80,000 posts reached 126 million users. Instagram saw 120,000 posts, while Twitter hosted thousands of fake accounts. These efforts garnered massive engagement, manipulating public opinion and fueling division.

Author: David Brand
